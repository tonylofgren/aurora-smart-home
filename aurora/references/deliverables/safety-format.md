# Safety Format

Format for `HAZARD-ANALYSIS.md` — the safety artifact Vera produces for every
hardware project that triggers her Iron Law 1 review criteria. Used by Vera
under Iron Law 2. Volt's pre-delivery disk check (Iron Law 8) confirms this
file exists before declaring delivery.

## When this file is required

HAZARD-ANALYSIS.md is required for any project with:
- A lithium battery (Li-ion, LiPo) or LiFePO4 cell
- An actuator controlling mains voltage (relay, TRIAC, SSR driving 100–240V)
- Outdoor mounting (rain, UV, thermal cycling exposure)
- Any supply or switched voltage > 5V DC or AC mains exposure

## File location

`<project>/hardware/HAZARD-ANALYSIS.md`

Always in the `hardware/` subdirectory, alongside SCHEMATIC.md, PCB-NOTES.md,
and BOM.md when those are present.

## Severity definitions

| Level | Definition |
|-------|------------|
| **Catastrophic** | Fire, structural damage, serious injury or death |
| **Major** | Significant property damage, injury requiring medical attention, battery thermal runaway |
| **Moderate** | Minor burn, equipment damage, data loss, smoke without fire |
| **Minor** | Temporary discomfort, cosmetic damage, non-critical equipment failure |

## Likelihood definitions

| Level | Definition |
|-------|------------|
| **Certain** | Will happen in normal use (e.g. relay contacts wear out over years) |
| **Likely** | More likely than not over the product's service life |
| **Possible** | Could happen under foreseeable misuse or component failure |
| **Unlikely** | Requires two or more independent failures simultaneously |
| **Rare** | Theoretically possible but no credible failure path |

## Common hazards by trigger category

### Battery (Li-ion / LiPo)

| Hazard | Typical severity | Common mitigation |
|--------|-----------------|-------------------|
| Overcharge → thermal runaway | Catastrophic | Charge IC with OVP (e.g. TP4056 + DW01A) |
| Overdischarge → cell damage | Moderate | Low-voltage cutoff in firmware + BMS |
| Mechanical puncture | Major | Rigid enclosure, no sharp edges near cell |
| Heat exposure (> 60°C) | Major | Enclosure ventilation, thermal barrier |
| Sealed enclosure + off-gas | Catastrophic | Vent holes >= 2 mm diameter, not facing down |

### Actuator (mains relay / TRIAC)

| Hazard | Typical severity | Common mitigation |
|--------|-----------------|-------------------|
| Relay contact stuck on | Major–Catastrophic | Normally-open relay for heating loads; physical override |
| Mains exposed on PCB | Catastrophic | >= 6 mm creepage, conformal coating, IP20 minimum |
| No overcurrent protection | Major | Fuse on mains side, rated for load |
| GPIO driving relay directly | Major | Relay driver (BJT / MOSFET) with flyback diode |

### Outdoor mounting

| Hazard | Typical severity | Common mitigation |
|--------|-----------------|-------------------|
| Water ingress → short | Major | IP65 minimum, gland-sealed cable entry |
| UV degradation of enclosure | Moderate | UV-stabilised ABS or polycarbonate |
| Condensation on PCB | Moderate | Conformal coating, desiccant pack |
| Temperature cycling fatigue | Moderate | Through-hole solder joints for vibration-prone connectors |

### High voltage (> 5V DC or AC mains)

| Hazard | Typical severity | Common mitigation |
|--------|-----------------|-------------------|
| Touch-accessible live parts | Catastrophic | Insulated enclosure, no exposed conductors |
| Inadequate clearance | Major | >= 6 mm creepage / >= 4 mm clearance at mains |
| No fuse | Major | Fuse rated for load, on live conductor |
| Earth fault path | Catastrophic | RCD/GFCI upstream (user responsibility to verify) |
