# Water Leak Sensor Example

> *Generated by [aurora@aurora-smart-home (esphome skill)](https://github.com/tonylofgren/aurora-smart-home)*

Battery-powered water leak detector that sleeps for years and screams the moment water arrives.

The device deep sleeps with all peripherals off, drawing about 30 uA. When water bridges a pair of stainless steel probes, the wake pin goes HIGH, the ESP32-C3 boots in roughly 300 ms, connects to WiFi, fires a notification to Home Assistant, and sounds a local piezo buzzer. Combined with a 2000 mAh LiPo, expected battery life is over two years.

## Features

- **Deep sleep always-on detection**: Sleeps indefinitely, wakes only on the leak event itself (no polling)
- **Immediate notification**: HA push notification within a few seconds of the first drop
- **Local piezo alarm**: Audible buzzer fires before the WiFi handshake completes
- **Battery monitoring**: Daily heartbeat reports voltage and remaining percent
- **Offline detection**: HA alerts you if the daily heartbeat is missed for 36+ hours
- **Tiny footprint**: Whole device fits in a 50 x 30 x 20 mm enclosure

## Hardware List

### Controller
- Seeed Studio Xiao ESP32-C3 (USB-C, integrated LiPo charger, JST-PH battery connector on back)

### Sensing
| Component | Purpose | Notes |
|-----------|---------|-------|
| Stainless steel probes (2x) | Water contact | M3 bolts, gold-plated header pins, or carbon rods |
| 1 MOhm resistor | Pull-down on sense pin | Keeps wake pin LOW when dry |
| Piezo buzzer | Local alarm | Active buzzer 3.3V, or passive with `rtttl` component |
| LiPo battery | Power | 2000 mAh recommended; Xiao charges via USB-C |
| Resistors 100k (2x) | Battery voltage divider | Optional but recommended |

### Optional Probe Variants
- **Commercial water sensor strip** (search "water leak detection cable"): drop-in replacement for the two probes, lets you snake the sensor along the floor under a dishwasher or hot water tank
- **Float switch**: for sump pits, replace the probes with a normally-open float switch wired between 3.3V and the wake pin

## Wiring Diagram

```
Xiao ESP32-C3
|
+-- 3V3 ---------- Probe A (always energized)
|
+-- GPIO3 -------- Probe B ----+
|                              |
|                              +-- 1 MOhm -- GND   (pull-down keeps pin LOW when dry)
|
+-- GPIO4 -------- Piezo buzzer (+) -- GND
|
+-- GPIO2 -------- 100k -- VBAT
|        |
|        +------- 100k -- GND        (voltage divider for battery monitoring)
|
+-- BAT+ / BAT- -- LiPo battery (JST-PH on back of Xiao)
```

When dry, GPIO3 is held LOW by the 1 MOhm pull-down. When water bridges Probe A to Probe B, current flows through the water, GPIO3 rises to 3.3V, and the ESP32-C3 wakes from deep sleep.

## Installation

1. **Generate secrets**:
   ```bash
   cp secrets.yaml.example secrets.yaml
   # edit secrets.yaml with your WiFi, API key and OTA password
   ```

2. **Flash the device** (USB-C cable to Xiao):
   ```bash
   esphome run water-leak-sensor.yaml
   ```
   After the first flash, subsequent updates happen wirelessly via OTA, but the device must be awake. Toggle the **Prevent Sleep** switch in HA before pushing OTA updates.

3. **Wire the probes**:
   - Solder Probe A to the 3V3 pin
   - Solder Probe B to GPIO3
   - Solder the 1 MOhm pull-down between GPIO3 and GND
   - Solder the buzzer between GPIO4 and GND
   - Plug the LiPo into the JST-PH connector on the back

4. **Test before deploying**:
   - With the device awake and connected, drop a wet finger across both probes
   - The buzzer should sound within 100 ms and the `binary_sensor.water_leak_sensor_leak` entity should flip to `on`
   - Dry the probes; the entity returns to `off`

5. **Add automations**:
   Copy `automations.yaml` content into Home Assistant. Replace `notify.mobile_app_your_phone` with your actual notification target.

## Probe Placement Guide

### Material
- **Stainless steel**: best long-term. Resists corrosion, gives consistent readings.
- **Gold-plated header pins**: fine for indoor air, but plating wears off after a few wet events.
- **Bare copper**: avoid. Oxidizes quickly and can poison the water reading with green corrosion.
- **Carbon rods** (pencil leads): cheap and corrosion-proof, but break easily.

### Spacing
The water between the probes acts as a resistor. Spacing depends on water conductivity:
- **Tap water** (most homes): 5 to 10 mm between probes works well
- **Distilled or RO water**: 1 to 3 mm. Pure water is a poor conductor. If you're protecting a humidifier or aquarium top-up reservoir, mount the probes very close together.
- **Salty water** (dishwasher, washing machine drain): 10 to 20 mm is fine; conductivity is high.

### Mounting
- Mount the probes flush with the floor, not above it. A 2 mm puddle should bridge them.
- Place the device body 10 to 20 cm higher than the probes (on a wall or shelf) so the electronics stay dry while the probes get wet.
- For hot water tank pans, place one probe at the lowest point of the pan and one a few cm away.
- For dishwashers and washing machines, place the probes under the front-left foot (typical leak point) with the device body screwed to the cabinet sidewall.

## Configuration Tuning

### Sleep Behavior
The device sleeps for 24 hours, wakes briefly to send a battery heartbeat, then sleeps again. Adjust in `water-leak-sensor.yaml`:

```yaml
substitutions:
  heartbeat_interval: "24h"   # change to 12h, 48h, 7d, etc.
```

Shorter intervals give you faster offline detection but eat more battery. 24h is a good default.

### Wake Stay-Awake Window
After waking, the device stays on for 30 seconds before going back to sleep. This window lets HA receive the leak event, fetch sensor values, and apply OTA updates. Increase if your WiFi is slow to associate:

```yaml
deep_sleep:
  run_duration: 30s   # raise to 60s on weak WiFi
```

### Battery Calibration
The default percent calculation assumes a 3.3V to 4.2V LiPo range. If your cell is different (LiFePO4, two-cell, etc.) adjust the lambda in `sensor: battery_percent`.

## Battery Life Estimate

| Mode | Current | Duration per day |
|------|---------|------------------|
| Deep sleep | ~30 uA | 23h 59m |
| Awake (heartbeat) | ~80 mA | ~30 s |
| Awake (leak event) | ~80 mA | ~30 s (rare) |

Average daily consumption: roughly 1.4 mAh. A 2000 mAh LiPo gives more than **3 years** in theory; real-world WiFi reconnects, leakage, and self-discharge bring this to a conservative **2+ years** between recharges.

If the device wakes frequently due to false triggers (humid air, condensation on the probes), battery life drops sharply. Use the spacing and material guidance above to reduce false wakes.

## Troubleshooting

| Issue | Solution |
|-------|----------|
| Device never wakes on water | Verify 1 MOhm pull-down is installed; check probe continuity with a multimeter while wet |
| Device wakes constantly | Probes too close together for ambient humidity, or condensation forming; increase spacing or move location |
| Buzzer doesn't sound | Confirm buzzer is the active type (built-in oscillator) for the simple GPIO switch driver |
| Battery percent jumps around | Add a `median` filter to `battery_voltage` or take readings only just before sleep |
| OTA update fails | Toggle `switch.water_leak_sensor_prevent_sleep` to ON before pushing, then OFF after |
| Heartbeat never arrives | Device may have crashed during boot; reflash via USB-C cable |

## Safety Notes

- **Do not** use this device as the sole protection for high-value flooring or unattended properties. Always pair it with a smart shutoff valve and renter/landlord insurance.
- **Do not** submerge the electronics. Only the probes should touch water.
- **Replace the battery** every 3 years even if charge level looks fine. LiPo cells degrade with calendar age.
- **Test monthly**: dip the probes in a glass of water and confirm the HA notification arrives. The `switch.water_leak_sensor_buzzer` entity lets you verify the buzzer works without water.
